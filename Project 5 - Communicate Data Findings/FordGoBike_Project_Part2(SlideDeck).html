Users Ride pattern in Ford GoBike System¶

Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation, We wanted to look at the characteristics of Ford GoBike system rides that could influence the likelihood of a user being a subscriber to the Customer. This will help the Company to create strategies to develop their subscription base users.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset used for this exploratory analysis consists of monthly individual trip data from January to December 2019.
We gathered the data directly from Lyft System Data website and we performed some important steps of data cleaning to include only useful features in the exploratory analysis.

Distribution of Trips Duration¶

The Duration of Trips in the dataset take on a large range of values, from about 1 minutes at the lowest end, to about 1400 minutes at the highest end. We limited the data to only trips less then one hour this made the distribution skewed to the right with long tail up to 60 minutes.

Distribution of Trips Distance¶

For the distance variable the users tend to ride bikes within a distance of 10 kilometers, with an occurrence of significant amounts of outliers, limiting the data to show only trips less then 10 km gives a nice right skewed distribution with gradually decreasing frequencies.

User Types and Their Monthly Rides¶

Subscribers contributed about 80% of bike usage in 2019, while Consumers contributed about 11% in the same year. Most of of the rides are taken in period of String and Autumn for both Subscribers and Customers, except in the month of December where the usage peaked for Customers. This usage pattern is highly related with the weather.

Daily Ride Duration by User Type¶

Customers use the bike share system for a relatively long period of time on weekdays, with a significant increase on weekends. In the other hand, subscribers have sustained usage in weekdays with a gradual increase in weekends.

Hourly Usage on days of week for Customers and Subscribers¶

Both Customers and Subscribers use the bike share system colossally during commuting hours (07-09AM and 16-18PM) on weekdays.
Additionally, weekday and commute time greatly influences the likelihood of a trip being a subscriber. Particularly, if a bike ride is on a weekday, the trip is more likely to be made by a subscriber than a customer. Similarly, a trip during the commute hours is more likely to be from a subscriber than not. With this information, we say that during weekdays and commute hours, there demand will be at a consistent level due to the subscribers tending to be the most frequent users.
